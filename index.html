Frequently Asked Questions About DemandLetter.Legal

What are your legal credentials?

We are a group of licensed attorneys, fully accredited by several US State Bars and with decades of legal experience. We have deep expertise in areas such as contracts, real estate, employment and family law.

Will I be able to review the Demand Letter prior to you sending?

Absolutely, we will provide a draft of the letter for your review prior to sending. We work closely with clients to make sure the letters are factually correct and as compelling as possible.

Will the recipient know that I am using your service?

Your letter will come directly from the attorney’s letterhead and the recipient will NOT have any knowledge of your use of DemandLetter.Legal. The purpose of the letter is to show you are serious about the matter and have every intent of pursuing further legal actions should they not meet your demands. They will assume you have gone through the expense of hiring an attorney to handle the entire case.

Does sending a Demand Letter mean I am suing someone in court?

this type of letter, even when coming from an attorney, is not a legal action that will initiate a lawsuit. It is simply a formal notice demanding that the person to whom the letter is addressed perform the action you believe they are obligated to do, such as paying a sum of money owed to you. This is a common step taken prior to formal litigation.

Will you handle any further correspondence on my behalf?

The scope of this service is only the sending of the initial letter. This alone may initiate a successful resolution to your dispute. However, if more action is required, that would require a different engagement with one of our attorneys or your own.

What happens when the letter is responded to?

Any responses to the letter, positive or negative, will be promptly forwarded to you so you can decide on the appropriate next course of action. This is included in the service cost at no extra charge.

Will you pay for postage?

Yes, it is all inclusive for the sending of one letter via USPS Priority Mail to any recipient in the United States. Express delivery will involve an additional charge.
